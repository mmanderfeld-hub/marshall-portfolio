Single Flange Caster Redesign

Role: Mechanical Engineering Intern

Company: Wilson Trailer Company

Term: Summer 2025

Overview

During my Summer 2025 internship at Wilson Trailer, I was tasked with redesigning an oversized single flange caster. The goal was to reduce overall size while preserving the functional flange and rolling surface geometry required for proper performance.

Engineering Process

I began by reverse-engineering the existing component, extracting critical dimensions and recreating the design in CAD. After establishing a fully parametric model, the geometry was scaled down while maintaining the necessary functional surfaces and mounting constraints.

To validate the updated design before committing to machining, I created a lightweight shell model for rapid prototyping. The 3D printed prototype allowed for dimensional verification and fit testing while production planning continued.

Manufacturing Outcome

After confirming proper fit and geometry through prototyping, the final design was manufactured by a local machining company. This ensured precision production of the optimized component while minimizing development risk.

Tools Used

  • Autodesk Inventor
  • Precision Measurement Tools
  • 3D Printing (Rapid Prototyping)
  • Vendor Coordination for Machining
