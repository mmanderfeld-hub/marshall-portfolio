Removable Arm Assembly for 45 Ton Brake Press

Role: Mechanical Engineering Intern

Company: Wilson Trailer Company

Term: Summer 2025

Design details have been limited to respect company confidentiality.

Overview

Designed and implemented a removable arm assembly for a 45-ton brake press to improve tooling access, maintain efficiency, and operator safety. Collaborated with the lead manufacturing engineer and machine operators to balance structural requirements with usability needs. Integrated a foldable support leg for stability and compact storage. Also utilized nylon ball transfers to prevent surface damage while enabling multi-axis material positioning.

Technical Contributions

  • Mechanical design and prototyping
  • 3D modeling and fit-up verification
  • Manufacturing support
  • Assembly and testing

Challenges

Balancing structural rigidity and removability while integrating into existing brake press geometry. The assembly required added support for stability, but the support system also had to fold or detach for storage. Additional constraints included preventing material surface damage and maintaining operator-friendly ergonomics.

What I Learned

Strengthened my ability to design within real-world manufacturing constraints, while translating operator and engineer feedback into practical mechanical solutions. Gained experience designing for servicability, ergonomics, and structural stability in an industrial environment.
